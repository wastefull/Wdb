## Overview

WasteDB respects the intellectual property rights of authors, publishers, and copyright holders. If you believe content on WasteDB infringes your copyright, this document explains how to request removal or redaction.

**Email:** compliance@wastefull.org  
**Form:** [/legal/takedown](#) (web form available 24/7)

## ðŸ› ï¸ Resolution Options

Upon review, WasteDB will take one of the following actions:

### **1. Full Removal**

- Entire MIU deleted from database
- Snippet, screenshot, and metadata removed
- Aggregations recalculated without this MIU
- Public Evidence tab updated immediately

**When Applied:**

- Clearly excessive quotation (>250 words)
- Entire figure/table reproduced
- Copyright owner provides no fair use exception

---

### **2. Partial Redaction**

- Snippet replaced with `[Redacted per copyright holder request - YYYY-MM-DD]`
- Screenshot removed
- Numeric value and metadata preserved (facts not copyrightable)
- Aggregations unchanged (derived statistics retained)
- Source citation remains with link to original

**When Applied:**

- Snippet removal sufficient to address concern
- Numeric fact non-infringing but context problematic
- Preserves scientific record while respecting copyright

---

### **3. Attribution Correction**

- Citation corrected to proper format
- Additional attribution added per copyright holder request
- "Copyright Â© [Year] [Holder]" notice added

**When Applied:**

- Content use is fair, but attribution inadequate
- Copyright holder requests specific citation format

---

### **4. No Action (Fair Use Defense)**

- Content remains online
- Detailed explanation provided to requester
- Appeal process available

**When Applied:**

- Snippet <250 words, properly cited, educational purpose
- Screenshot low-resolution, used for scientific verification
- Transformative use (data extraction, not reproduction)
- No market harm to original work

**Appeal Process:**

- Copyright holder may submit counter-arguments
- WasteDB will reconsider with additional context
- Final decision within 14 days

## ðŸ”„ Counter-Notification (For Curators)

If you uploaded content that was removed, and you believe removal was in error:

1. **Submit counter-notification:** compliance@wastefull.org
2. **Include:**

   - Your contact information
   - MIU ID of removed content
   - Statement: "I have a good faith belief that material was removed due to mistake or misidentification."
   - Statement: "I consent to jurisdiction of Federal District Court."
   - Statement: "I will accept service of process from copyright claimant."
   - Your signature

3. **Timeline:**
   - WasteDB forwards counter-notice to original requester
   - If no lawsuit filed within 14 days, content may be restored
   - Final decision at WasteDB discretion

## âš ï¸ Repeat Infringer Policy

**Three-Strike Policy:**

If a curator's content is subject to **3 valid takedown requests**:

1. **Strike 1:** Warning + required training on fair use
2. **Strike 2:** Temporary suspension (30 days)
3. **Strike 3:** Permanent curation privileges revoked

**Appeals:** Available at each strike level via compliance@wastefull.org

**Purpose:** Protect WasteDB from repeat infringers while supporting curators who make good faith mistakes.
